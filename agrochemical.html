Agrochemicals

Definition

An agrochemical or agrichemical, a contraction of agricultural chemical, is a chemical product used in agriculture. In most cases, agrichemical refers to pesticides including insecticides, herbicides, fungicides and nematicides.

Applications:

  • Used as fertilizers.
  • It is used as Liming and acidifying agents.
  • Used as Soil conditioners.
  • Also as Pesticides.
  • Agrochemicals is also used for animal husbandry.

Features:

  • This are chemical compounds used for promoting plant growth.
  • Pesticide is a chemical or a substance used to destroy or control some types of plants or organisms also known as pests, which are harmful to
    cultivated plants or to animals. Pesticides mostly work through poisoning pests
