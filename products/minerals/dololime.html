Dololime (CaO, MgO)

Dololime (CaO, MgO)

Definition

Dololime is mainly produced through the heating of Dolomite rocks. The process may entail some selected special sizes that undergo high temperature ranging between 9000C & 11000C in specialized Kilns.

Ca, Mg (CO3)2 + HEAT -----> CaO, MgO + CO2

In this process, Calcium Carbonate and Magnesium Carbonate will get disintegrated, and then turned into Calcium Oxide and Magnesium Oxide, as well as Carbon Di-Oxide which is released. The produced Dololime is then broken down into special sizes fitting the needs of various ind ustries.

Properties:

  • Its melting point is very high 15000 C thats why used in wall furnace.
  • It contain 30-35% Magnesium Oxide and 60-65% Calcium Oxide.
  • Small amounts of iron in the structure give the crystals a yellow to brown tint.
  • Because dololime can be dissolved by slightly acidic water, areas of dolomite are important as aquifers and contribute to karst terrain formation.
  • Unless it is in fine powder form, it does not rapidly dissolve or effervesce (fizz) in cold dilute hydrochloric acid as calcite does.

Applications:

  • It is used as a source of magnesia (MgO), a feed additive for livestock, a sintering agent and flux in metal processing, and as an ingredient in the production of glass, bricks, and ceramics.
  • It is used in iron and steel making.
  • It plays a vital role in wastewater treatement.
  • Frequently used in production of heat refractories.
  • It is also used in production of Magnesium

Features:

  • Dololime is also used as the substrate in marine (saltwater) aquariums to help buffer changes in the pH of the water.
  • In horticulture, dolomite and dolomitic limestone are added to soils and soilless potting mixes as a pH buffer and as a magnesium source.
  • In addition to being an industrial mineral, dolomite is highly valued by collectors and museums when it forms large, transparent crystals.
