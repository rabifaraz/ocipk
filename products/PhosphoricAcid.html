Phosphoric acid

Phosphoric acid

Definition

Phosphoric acid, also known as orthophosphoric acid or phosphoric(V) acid, is a weak acid with the chemical formula H ₃PO ₄. It is a non-toxic compound which, when pure, is a solid at room temperature and pressure. All three hydrogens are acidic to varying degrees and can be lost from the molecule as H⁺ ions.

Properties:

  • Solubility: Soluble in ethanol
  • Chemical formula: H3PO4
  • Molar mass: 97.994 g·mol−1
  • Appearance: Colorless crystalline solid
  • Odor: Odorless
  • Density: 1.6845 g⋅cm−3 (25 °C, 85%), 1.834 g⋅cm−3 (solid)
  • Melting point: 42.4 °C (108.3 °F; 315.5 K)
  • Boiling point: 407 °C (765 °F; 680 K)

Applications:

  • In anti-rust treatment by phosphate conversion coating or passivation
  • As an external standard for phosphorus-31 nuclear magnetic resonance.
  • In phosphoric acid fuel cells.
  • In activated carbon production.
  • In compound semiconductor processing, to etch Indium gallium arsenide selectively with respect to indium phosphide.
  • In microfabrication to etch silicon nitride selectively with respect to silicon dioxide.
  • As a pH adjuster in cosmetics and skin-care products.
  • As a sanitizing agent in the dairy, food, and brewing industries.

Features:

  • When phosphoric acid is dissolved in a superacid, poorly characterized products are formed.
  • Phosphoric acid also has the potential to contribute to the formation of kidney stones, especially in those who have had kidney stones previously.
  • In classical endocrine regulation, low serum phosphate induces the renal production of the seco-steroid hormone 1, 25-dihydroxyvitamin D3 (1, 25(OH)2D3).
  • Green phosphoric acid is prepared by adding sulphuric acid to calcium phosphate rock, or slurry.
